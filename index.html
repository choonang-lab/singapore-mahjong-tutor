How scoring works

Shanten = how many tile-swaps from a winning hand (0 = ready to win). Acceptance (ukeire) = the number of live tiles that would improve your hand after a discard. The best discard is the one that keeps you at the lowest shanten with the most accepting tiles. Flowers, seasons and animals are bonus tiles and never appear in the hand.

v1 uses standard hands only (4 sets + 1 pair) and counts remaining tiles from your hand alone. Opponent discards come in a later module.

The animal & flower system

Bonus tiles are set aside (not part of your 4 sets + pair) and replaced with a fresh draw. They give value two ways: hand tai (collected only if you win) and instant payouts (paid immediately by every opponent the moment they appear — the Singapore signature).

Flowers (Plum 1, Orchid 2, Chrysanthemum 3, Bamboo 4) and seasons (Spring 1, Summer 2, Autumn 3, Winter 4) are numbered 1–4. Your seat number (East 1, South 2, West 3, North 4) matches one flower and one season — those are “yours”.

Animals 🐱🐭🐓🐛 pay a tai each. The predator–prey pairs Cat + Rat and Rooster + Centipede each trigger an instant payout; all four pay more still.

Every amount is house-dependent — tune them on the ⚙ Rules screen. Instant amounts are per-opponent units.

Reading safety

Genbutsu 現物 — a tile already in an opponent's discards is 100% safe against them: the furiten rule forbids winning on a tile you've discarded. This is the bedrock of defense.

Suji 筋 — a two-sided (ryanmen) wait covers two tiles three apart (e.g. 4–7). If West discarded the 4, a 5-6 shape waiting 4–7 would be furiten, so the 7 is safer. Suji only rules out two-sided waits — a tanki, shanpon or kanchan can still hit it, so it's likely, not guaranteed.

v1 defends against one opponent from their discard river. Wall-count (“no-chance”) reads, multiple opponents, and push-vs-fold EV come later. Discarding a genbutsu is always the optimal answer here.
